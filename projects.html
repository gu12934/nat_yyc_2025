Neural.ly - Accessible Neurofeedback

The Challenge: Despite the amazing therapeutic potential of neurofeedback, accessibility is heavily limited. Neurofeedback programs are expensive and often require patients to go to a facility with specialized equipment.

Our Solution: Our project began with the simple goal of making neurofeedback more convenient in conjunction with a newly increased availability of loanable EEG devices. We designed a web application that caters to the needs of both patients and providers.

Innovation Highlights:

  • Web-based platform for remote neurofeedback therapy
  • Integration with loanable EEG devices for accessibility
  • User-friendly interface designed in Figma for patients and providers
  • Breaking down cost and location barriers to mental health treatment

BraVRy - VR Exposure Therapy

The Problem: Specific phobias afflict nearly 10% of Canadian adults, rivaling the prevalence of age-related hearing loss. The burden of anxiety disorders collectively surpasses that of cancer, highlighting the pressing need for advanced interventions.

The Solution: BraVRy presents an innovative solution aimed at revolutionizing the treatment of specific phobias and PTSD by integrating cutting-edge VR technology with comprehensive biotracking.

Key Features:

  • Immersive VR Simulations: State-of-the-art VR experiences inspired by the proven effectiveness of exposure therapy
  • Comprehensive Biotracking: Monitors heart rate, blood pressure, breathing, brain activity, and body movement through accelerometer and gyroscope
  • Customizable Environments: Doctor's office within VR space facilitates diverse and adaptable patient experiences
  • Analytics Dashboard: Visual tracking and analysis of biometric measures throughout treatment courses

Impact: BraVRy seeks to alleviate the economic burden of current anxiety disorder treatments, which cost Canada's healthcare system over $6.8 billion in 2022, while significantly enhancing patient outcomes.

Innovation: Seamlessly synthesizes biological measures with immersive technology, representing a breakthrough in comprehending and addressing anxiety disorders. This approach aligns with scientific literature showing the superiority of VR exposure therapies in treating phobias and PTSD.
